## Executive Summary
In mid-2026, the Board of Directors (Connor, Greg, Eric, Ken, and Hunter) formally proposed to exit the legacy warehouse space at 130 W. Lachapelle. The primary goal of the move was to solve major environmental and infrastructure roadblocks that were causing membership churn, and relocate to a more sustainable space at 8610 Botts Ln.

## Rationale for Moving
The decision to leave Lachapelle was driven by several systemic issues outside of the organization's control:
- **Temperature:** Extreme Texas heat lacking adequate climate control.
- **Space Layout:** Inability to run classes at scale due to the lack of separation between dusty shop work (wood/metal) and sensitive equipment (clean room/electronics).
- **Power Infrastructure:** Low-quality incoming power causing machine unreliability and preventing certain high-draw tools from being connected.
- **Building Quality:** Metal-shed construction limiting aesthetic/comfort improvements, security vulnerabilities, and ongoing battles with flooding, pests, brownouts, and costly water leaks.
- **Financial Constraints:** Rent had increased from $0.38/SF/MO to $1.00/SF/MO, bringing the organization to exactly break-even despite growing the membership base.

## The Proposal to Landlord (Alex)
Because 10BitWorks mistakenly signed a two-year lease extending through March 2027 without an early termination clause, the Board formally proposed a mutual early termination:
1. **Effective Date:** Mutual termination effective July 31, 2026.
2. **Infrastructure Kept:** 10BitWorks agreed to leave behind all the substantial 110/220v electrical infrastructure installed over the 9-year tenure.
3. **Delivery:** Broom-clean delivery with rent current through July.

## The New Facility
The new lease agreement for **8610 Botts Ln** was drafted in May 2026. This location was selected for better infrastructure, appropriate zoning for maker/fabrication use, and room in the operational budget.
