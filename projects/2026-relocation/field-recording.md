# Makerspace Relocation and Lease Negotiation Strategy
*Created on Apr 19, 2026 16:21:38*

## Short Summary
The board discussed strategies to break their current lease due to maintenance failures and move to a better space.

## Long Summary
You and several board members discussed the urgent need to relocate the makerspace due to poor property conditions, including flooding, electrical issues, and a lack of separate metering. You reviewed the current lease, which runs until March 2027, and strategized on how to negotiate an early termination with the landlord, Alex. The plan involves documenting a long history of broken promises and maintenance failures to gain leverage. You also explored the possibility of rebranding or reorganizing the nonprofit to better fulfill its educational mission.


# Lease and Potential Relocation Strategy

- **Decision on New Space**
  - The board has selected "Bots" as the preferred new location.
  - A Letter of Intent (LOI) has been agreed upon, but signing is on hold until the current lease situation is resolved.
  - "Bots" offers central air conditioning, better bathrooms, and overall better amenities for approximately $0.80–$0.85 per square foot (triple netted), compared to $1.00 at the current location.
- **Current Lease Status**
  - The current lease at West Law Chapel is set to expire on March 31, 2027.
  - Terminating now would theoretically cost around $40,000 in remaining rent.
  - The landlord (Alex) previously provided a $1,000 monthly discount, which has recently expired or been unilaterally continued by the tenant through deductions.
- **Negotiation Strategy with Landlord (Alex)**
  - **Notice of Non-Renewal:** Formally notify Alex that the lease will not be renewed in 2027.
  - **Early Termination:** Propose a mutual termination (e.g., for July 31) to allow the landlord to find a higher-paying tenant.
  - **Leveraging Maintenance Issues:** Document a history of "broken promises" and property defects to negotiate a lower exit fee or early release.
  - **Legal Consultation:** A lawyer (Mark) has reviewed the lease; while there is no explicit "out" clause, documentation of landlord breaches (plumbing, safety, etc.) may provide leverage.

# Property Maintenance and Facility Issues

- **Plumbing and Sewage**
  - The system is "daisy-chained" across multiple tenants (Cheerleaders, Glass company, 10 Bit Works) using sump pumps.
  - Frequent sewage overflows and backups have occurred, creating health and safety concerns.
  - A major water leak (not caused by the tenant) resulted in a $3,000 bill; saws/city provided a 50% credit, but the tenant still absorbed a $1,500 loss.
- **Electrical and Utilities**
  - Lack of separate meters for water and electricity remains a primary grievance.
  - 10 Bit Works is currently paying for electricity that powers exterior lighting for other buildings.
  - Promised electrical upgrades and separate panels never materialized.
- **Structural and Safety Concerns**
  - **Roofing:** A large metal panel on the roof is loose and "slams" during storms, posing a potential lethal hazard if it detaches.
  - **Flooding:** Recent storms resulted in water entering the ceramics room due to poor drainage and the removal of previous shielding.
  - **Security:** The property is vulnerable due to the adjacent train tracks; the fence has been breached multiple times (e.g., the "tire incident" where 30 tires were stolen).

# Membership and Financial Health

- **Revenue Trends**
  - Membership typically drops during the summer months due to the lack of air conditioning in the warehouse.
  - Current revenue is just enough to cover the new $1.00/sq ft rate, but any membership decline would result in a deficit.
- **Non-Profit Growth and Staffing**
  - Discussion regarding the need to transition from a "club" feel to a professional educational institution.
  - Goal to hire staff (specifically a fundraising/grant writer) to scale operations.
  - Exploring SBA grants and vocational education funding related to "Made in America" initiatives for job training.

# Operational Actions and Documentation

- **Compendium of Grievances**
  - Action item for Greg and Connor to create a Google Doc listing all historical maintenance issues, unfulfilled verbal agreements, and text-based promises from Alex and his property manager (Ryan).
  - Search Slack and Google Voice archives for documentation of reported leaks, plumbing issues, and requests for separate meters.
- **Landlord Communications**
  - A formal letter from the Board is to be drafted and sent (likely Tuesday).
  - The tone should remain "polite but firm," framing the exit as a matter of organizational survival and the space's inability to meet their 501(c)(3) mission.
