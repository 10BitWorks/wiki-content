Hey Sam,

I spent some time diving into the fine print of that lease draft for 8610 Botts Ln. Overall, it’s looking pretty good, but there are a handful of things in the boilerplate that really caught my eye. Since you're heading to the table tomorrow, I wanted to shoot over some thoughts on what we should push back on. 

A lot of this is standard "landlord-friendly" stuff, but for a group like ours, it’s a bit risky. Here’s what I’m thinking:

### 1. The HVAC Maintenance Monopoly (Section 15E)
They checked the box saying we **must** have a regularly scheduled maintenance contract. Honestly, since we’re accepting the system "as-is" and we are on the hook for 100% of the replacement cost if it dies, it feels wrong for them to dictate who we hire. We've got members who can handle filter changes and coil cleanings in their sleep. 
*   **Suggestion:** Check the "is NOT required" box. We’ll take the liability, but we need the autonomy to manage it ourselves.

### 2. The "Volunteer" Reality Check (Notice Periods)
Right now, a lot of the deadlines (like restoring the security deposit or fixing minor repairs) are set at **10 days**. Since we’re a volunteer board, we aren't at the space every single day to check the physical mailbox. If a letter sits there for a week, we’re effectively in default before we even open it.
*   **Suggestion:** Change all "10-day" windows to **30 days**. Also, can we make sure all notices are sent via **Email** as well as Certified Mail? The clock shouldn't start until we actually see it.

### 3. Killing the "3-Day Hammer" (Section 20C)
The "Acceleration of Rent" clause is honestly terrifying. A single missed payment or technical default shouldn't give them the right to demand all 5 years of rent at once. Most non-profits would just go under instantly if that happened.
*   **Suggestion:** Strike the acceleration clause entirely. Also, let's limit that 3-day notice to **non-payment of rent only**, which matches Texas law anyway.

### 4. The "Modern Edge Security" Issue (Section 35)
This one is a total non-starter for us. They snuck in a bunch of bullet points forcing us to use a specific vendor for all cameras, data drops, and access control. 
As a hackerspace, we *build* this stuff. We already have a network-isolated CCTV system (Frigate) and our own custom access control that’s built into our membership database. We don't want proprietary, cloud-tethered stuff that we have to pay a monthly fee for.
*   **Suggestion:** Strike everything about Modern Edge. We’ll agree to return the walls to original condition when we leave, but we need to manage our own tech.

### 5. Protecting Member Gear (Section 23)
The Landlord’s Lien technically gives them a claim to all the property in the building. The problem is, about half the expensive tools in there belong to individual members, not the organization. I don't want a landlord trying to "handcuff" a member's CNC or laser because of a rent dispute.
*   **Suggestion:** Strike the lien or explicitly exclude "member-owned tools and equipment."

### 6. The $100 Bounced Check Fee (Section 4H)
$100 for a returned check feels like a pure penalty. My own bank doesn't even charge half that.
*   **Suggestion:** Change it to "actual bank fee plus a $25 admin fee." It’s a small thing, but every bit counts for us.

Let me know what you think. I’m not speaking officially for the board yet, just wanted to give you my personal take so you have some ammo for tomorrow.

Thanks again for all the help with this!
