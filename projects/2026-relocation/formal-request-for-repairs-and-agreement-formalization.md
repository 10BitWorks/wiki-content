May 3, 2026

Alex Bahrami  

**Sent via Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested**

Dear Alex,

We are writing to you today to discuss several pressing facility matters at 130 W. Lachapelle and to touch base on our ongoing arrangements. 

First, thanks again for the 12-month corporate membership / sponsorship donation you’ve provided over the last year. As this agreement comes to a close this month, we’ve been reviewing our records and noticed that there were two months last year where the sponsorship funds were not received. To make it easier for you and to keep our internal bookkeeping clean as we wrap up the agreement, we plan to apply those remaining credits to our rent payments over the next two months.

Regarding the building itself, the record-breaking rainfall last weekend — where more rain fell on Saturday than any day in the last 13 years — brought some long-standing structural vulnerabilities into sharp focus. We’ve observed substantial water intrusion under the rear wall, something we had mitigated over 5 years ago (with a makeshift solution by member Joe Rogers), but was removed by your Property Manager Ryan during the setup of the new subdivision. The replacement solution he installed, if any, is significantly less performant, as our back hallway and ceramics area were significantly inundated — with water reaching nearly to the front door. We're asking for a professional inspection to restore the building’s exterior integrity, especially as we continue to manage the hazardous corrugated panel on the upper roof that is now almost completely detached and slamming into the structure during these windstorms.

The recent subdivision of the warehouse has also created some difficult logistical hurdles. The installation of plumbing for our new neighbors has physically obstructed the entrance to the shed housing our water heater. The unit is currently broken, and because the new lines prevent the shed door from opening, we have no way to access it for repairs without resorting to cutting down the door itself. We would like to have those lines adjusted so we can repair our heater and finally make use of the facility’s hot water plumbing. Additionally, we’ve found that the current wastewater configuration, which routes waste from adjacent units through daisy-chained sump pumps into our sewage line, is reaching its mechanical limit and creating recurring maintenance friction that we would prefer to resolve by isolating our plumbing lines.

Lastly, we would like to request the promised installation of independent electric meters, a goal the organization has been discussing with you since we first moved in back in 2017. The current shared arrangement continues to be a source of significant operational risk. Ideally, the building's power would enter separately for the separate units, the arrangement Ryan said he planned back when they put up the new wall. Electrical upgrades and diagnosis have necessarily involved coordinating with the Cheerleader school in the adjacent 130B unit where our main power panel is still located. That's been more accessible now that the unit is vacant, but the placement continues to cause issues, such as the one we recently contacted you about where your maintenance contractor Luis entered the locked space on March 30th while our equipment was still there and removed all of it, including our best ladder, taking it to an unknown location. While he acknowledged taking the items, he expressed limited ability to communicate in English and is relying on you to relay the message. We’ve also noted that the other neighboring unit (Glass cutting / Window installation company) is currently being lit by the 20+ high-quality LED fixtures our members spent weeks installing on scaffolding. While Ryan said he'd take these down to return to us, this never happened before the new tenant moved in. These are still wired to our meter, meaning we are effectively paying to light their space — even when they're not around, since we can't turn them off without also turning off lights on our side.

If you're still considering our earlier proposal for a July exit, it's worth noting that units 130 (currently 10BitWorks) and 130b (former cheerleader school) share so much intertwined infrastructure that removing the partition between them would not only be significantly less work than properly separating them, but would form a cohesive single unit with Central Air, large exhaust fans, sensible power distribution, 2 bay doors, and a generally more attractive 10,000SF+ space for your future tenants.

Regardless, we look forward to hearing your thoughts on a timeline for these repairs.

Sincerely,

The Board of Directors  
10BitWorks Makerspace
130 W. Lachapelle, San Antonio, TX 78204
