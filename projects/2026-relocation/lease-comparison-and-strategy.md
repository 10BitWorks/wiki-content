# Lease Comparison and Strategic Analysis
**To:** 10BitWorks Board of Directors
**From:** Connor, President
**Date:** April 19, 2026

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the critical shifts between our original 2017 Lease and the 2025 Current Lease. While the 2025 lease significantly increased our financial liability and maintenance responsibilities, it preserved specific Landlord obligations that form the core of our "Needy Tenant" strategy.

---

## 1. Key Contractual Shifts

| Feature | 2017 Lease | 2025 Current Lease |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Rent** | $2,900 (Gross) | $5,000 (NNN-style) |
| **Effective Rate** | ~$0.37 / sq ft | ~$1.00 / sq ft |
| **Space Size** | 7,800 sq ft (explicit) | ~5,000 sq ft (estimated/site plan) |
| **Landlord Repairs** | Exterior walls, roof, foundations, gutters. | **Roof and Exterior Walls ONLY.** |
| **Tenant Repairs** | Interior systems & HVAC. | **Everything** (Plumbing, HVAC, Electrical) **including CapEx.** |
| **Termination** | Lessor could terminate with 60d notice. | **No early termination clause.** |
| **Extension** | Contained 5-year options. | **Option to extend crossed out.** |

## 2. Supporting Clauses (Our Leverage)

The following sections support our claim that the Landlord is in breach or that we have a path to exit.

### **Landlord Responsibility for Structural Integrity**
**Section 13 (Landlord's Repairs):**
> *"Landlord shall have no obligations under this Lease to repair or maintain any portion of the Premises... other than the **Roof and Exterior Walls**."*

*   **Strategy:** We must aggressively document the "giant metal panel" roof hazard and the "Swiss cheese" walls. Contractually, these are Alex's only jobs, and he is failing them.

### **The "Needy Tenant" Trigger**
**Section 26 (Tenant's Remedies):**
> *"Landlord shall not be in default hereunder unless Landlord fails to perform any of its obligations hereunder within thirty (30) days after written notice from Tenant specifying such failure..."*

*   **Strategy:** By sending formal notice of the roof and wall issues now, we start a 30-day "cure" clock. If he fails to fix them (or realizes the cost of fixing them), it strengthens our position for mutual termination.

### **Mitigation of Damages**
**Section 25 (Landlord's Remedies):**
> *"...the excess of the then present value of the Base Rent... over the present value of any net amounts which Tenant establishes **Landlord can reasonably expect to recover by reletting the Premises**..."*

*   **Strategy:** Our attorney (Mark Weeden) confirms that if we vacate, Alex is legally required to attempt to re-let the space to "mitigate" his losses. He cannot simply sit on the empty space and collect $40k from us if a new tenant is available.

## 3. Weakening Clauses (Our Vulnerabilities)

The following sections are hurdles we must navigate carefully.

### **The "Pay Regardless" Rule**
**Section 31 (Independent Covenants):**
> *"Tenant agrees to perform all of its obligations hereunder (including... the obligation to pay rent), **irrespective of any breach or alleged breach by Landlord** of any such implied warranty."*

*   **Risk:** This is the most dangerous clause. It means we cannot legally withhold rent just because the roof is leaking or the walls are failing. We must pay while we negotiate.

### **Tenant Liability for Major Systems**
**Section 14 (Tenant's Repairs):**
> *"Tenant, at its expense, shall repair, replace and maintain... all areas, improvements and systems... including electric, plumbing, water and wastewater lines... **Such repairs and replacements include capital expenditures and repairs whose benefit may extend beyond the Lease Term.**"*

*   **Risk:** Alex can argue that the broken water heater and the plumbing capacity issues are *our* problem to fix, even if they cost $10k+ and benefit him long after we leave.

### **Security Disclaimer**
**Section 32 (No Security Service):**
> *"Landlord is not providing any security services... and Tenant waives any claim against Landlord with respect to any loss by theft or any other damage suffered... in connection with any unauthorized entry."*

*   **Risk:** This explicitly absolves Alex of the "tire incident" theft. We can only use the hole in the fence as a "maintenance failure" (Exterior Walls), not as a liability for the stolen property.

## 4. Proposed Action Plan
1.  **Notice of Structural Failure:** Immediately issue formal notice regarding the Roof (Safety Hazard) and Exterior Walls (Security/Weather Seal) per Section 13.
2.  **Inaccessibility Defense:** Argue that the Landlord’s improper PVC routing has "prevented" us from fulfilling our Section 14 obligations regarding the water heater.
3.  **The "Unified Message":** Send the proposed July 31st termination letter, couching the maintenance list as "The cost of keeping us versus the benefit of re-letting a combined, functional space."
