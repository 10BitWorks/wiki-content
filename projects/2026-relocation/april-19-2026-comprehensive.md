# Meeting Summary: April 19, 2026
**Comprehensive Overview for Hunter**

## 1. The Moving "Limbo"
The makerspace is currently in a state of financial and operational limbo. The board has reached a consensus to proceed with the **"Botts"** location and has a Letter of Intent (LOI) that both parties agree on. However, signing the new lease is considered financially reckless until a clear exit strategy from the current lease is established. The remaining commitment on the current lease is approximately 10–12 months, representing a potential liability of **$40,000**.

## 2. Lease & Landlord Strategy (Alex)
The board discussed a multi-pronged approach to negotiating with the landlord, Alex, focusing on the expiration of previous agreements and a mounting list of grievances.

### **Financial Arrangements and Revenue**
*   **The $1,000 Deduction:** Financial records confirm that the "handshake agreement" discount (which Alex initially framed as a $1,000/month membership subscription) transitioned into a direct rent deduction starting in **October 2025**. Rent payments dropped from ~$4,000 (e.g., $4,045.14 in Sept 2025) to ~$3,000 (e.g., $3,049.82 in Oct 2025). 
*   **One-Time Donation:** Records show Alex provided a one-time donation of **$1,000** on **August 19, 2025**, rather than the ongoing monthly subscription he originally proposed.
*   **Sustainability:** While current revenue (approx. $6,100 - $6,400 monthly from Stripe/Squarespace) can technically cover the full rent rate (~$4,250 base), the board noted that this is not sustainable during the summer. Historically, membership dips significantly when the warehouse becomes "blisteringly hot," as many members choose not to work in a space that is hotter than the outdoors.

### **The "Needy Tenant" and Leverage Strategy**
To encourage Alex to agree to a mutual termination on **July 31, 2026**, the board plans to shift from being an "easy" tenant to a "needy" one. The strategy is to demand that Alex fulfill his legal and promised obligations as a landlord. If he realizes that keeping 10BitWorks as a tenant will require significant capital expenditure, he may be more inclined to let them out of the lease.

### **Compendium of Broken Promises & Maintenance Issues**
Connor is compiling a formal document of unfulfilled promises and building failures to use as leverage. Mentioned items include:
*   **Utility Metering:** A long-standing promise of separate electric and water meters never materialized. The makerspace currently pays the bill for the entire warehouse and must manually calculate and deduct the neighbor's usage, which is a significant administrative burden.
*   **Water Leak Liability:** On **September 10, 2025**, the makerspace incurred a **$1,265.44 out-of-pocket loss** for a massive water bill caused by a neighbor's leak (approx. 80,000 gallons wasted). This amount was paid *after* receiving a 50% credit from the city.
*   **Structural Failures:** 
    *   **Roof:** A "giant metal panel" on the roof is unsecured and slams down rapidly onto the ceiling during storms, creating a potentially life-threatening hazard.
    *   **Walls:** The exterior walls were described as "Swiss cheese," failing to provide a proper seal for the building.
    *   **Water Heater:** The unit has been non-functional for several years, and plumbing additions sloppily routed a rigid PVC pipe straight across the opening to the shed it's housed in, completely obstructing the door so the unit is not accessible for repairs or replacement. The lack of hot water has caused backflow and leak issues in the bathroom plumbing.
*   **Plumbing and Sewage:** Sump pumps have been daisy-chained to support new tenants without increasing system capacity, leading to multiple instances of flooding and "sewage overflow incidents" on the block.
*   **Electrical and Lighting:** 
    *   Property manager Ryan promised separate power routing that was never completed.
    *   New lights in the adjacent space are wired to 10BitWorks' power, and the makerspace cannot manually recover their own lighting equipment from that space now that it is occupied.
*   **Security:** The "tire incident" highlighted the lack of security. Thieves cut a human-sized hole in the perimeter fence and stole 30 tires from a storage container in a single night.
*   **Hostile Neighbors:** **WarHouse Gym** frequently occupies shared parking spaces and blocks public trails, receiving seemingly "sweetheart" treatment from the landlord.
