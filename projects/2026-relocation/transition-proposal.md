Hi Alex,

Thanks for taking the time to talk through our situation last week with John and Greg. While you continue the discussion with John, we wanted to follow up in writing with some context on where we stand as an organization, a documented statement of our intention for the current lease, and a clear proposal for how we can meet each other's goals.

10BitWorks has kept going because of a community of members who donate time, skills, and money to the organization — operating without a single paid staff member. Yet, the organization maintaining that community has survived for over 15 years — even after the pandemic recession took the member count to new lows.

The Lachapelle warehouse has been that community's home for the better part of a decade, and we cannot overstate our appreciation for your stewardship and understanding. Increasingly, though, that same community has been telling us — through exits, declined memberships, and direct feedback — that the current space no longer works for them.

We — Connor, Greg, Eric, Ken, and Hunter — are Board members, but wear many other hats in the organization — and since our term started, have dedicated it especially toward capturing and addressing the common reasons members become ex-members. Wherever our volunteer force has the power to do something about it, we have. This was highly successful in stemming some of the bleeding: we're heading into summer with 20 more paid memberships than we started with, and the organization has nearly recovered its size from right before the pandemic (when Ken became the 100th member). 

While that would have gotten us new spending power and exciting possibilities back when we moved in at $0.38/SF/MO — at today’s rate of $1.00/SF/MO, even after increasing member dues, it means we break even. Luckily, we now have just enough revenue to remain solvent as your 1-year sponsorship expires, and we proceed with the lease. **We thank you for your sponsorship** \- that temporary agreement likely made the difference for the continued existence of 10BitWorks in 2026, and our appreciation can't be overstated.

The Texas summer is arriving, though, and soon to follow is the next inevitable wave of dropouts as our warehouse hits temperatures higher than the outdoors itself. While we're optimistically expecting better retention this time around, the cleanup, organization, beautification, support, and safety improvements can only get us so far. The best way for our nonprofit to sustain 3✕ higher rent than 2017 is to sustain 3✕ more committed members, but the growth rate required defies our history in this space. Assuming it's eventually possible, we simply *must* continue to address the big reasons members leave. But so long as we remain in this industrial warehouse, the reasons that remain are the ones not feasibly within our control.

In a nutshell: the heat through most of the year, the inability to run classes at scale, and the lack of separation between dusty shop work and sensitive equipment are actively costing us members. The setup and quality of the power infrastructure coming into our unit makes several of our machines unreliable, and leaves some machines unable to be hooked up at all. The metal-shed construction drastically constrains our ability to make the aesthetic improvements needed to be on par with the more comfortable environment other makerspaces provide, or to properly rectify the security issues we've been hit with. We tend to battle flooding, pests, brownouts, and costly water leaks, directing our volunteers' time and energy away from the things that get us new members. In full transparency: the organization has occasionally looked into relocating 10BitWorks in years past. But with this combination of factors today, it's no longer a matter of convenience for us — it's what we need to do to stay viable.

Thus, you hopefully understand our decision: Whether we remain solvent until March 2027 or not, **we will not be renewing our lease**.

With that official notice out of the way, we also have a proposal for you, that we strongly believe would guarantee that 10BitWorks lives to see its 16th birthday.

Here's what we'd like to propose:

1\.  Mutual termination of the lease effective July 31, 2026\. That extends past our new space's readiness date and **gives you two additional months** of runway to line up a replacement tenant, addressing the timing concern you raised.  
2\.  Our improvements stay with your building. Over the years we've added substantial 110/220 electrical infrastructure and outlet capacity throughout our footprint — the kind of build-out any future maker, fabrication, or light-industrial tenant will value; the kind that's expensive to add from scratch.  
3\.  **Broom-clean delivery** with all rent current through July 31\.  
4\.  Active **tenant referrals** from our network in the San Antonio maker, small-fabrication, and creative communities. We've surfaced qualified tenants for similar spaces before and are ready to put real effort into it here.

We also want to be straightforward with you: last year we made a mistake in committing to a two year lease rather than a single year, and in not negotiating a clear Early Termination clause like we had with our previous lease. We sincerely regret the position that puts you in.

By shopping outside of the city center, though, we've found some options that meet our needs, give us room in our budget, have agreed to our use case, and would appeal to our ex-members. We're excited about the potential here, and by negotiating an early termination with us, you would really help us out\! While we don't have the funds for a termination fee beyond the two months additional rent, we want to continue to increase the value of your building and do whatever we can to help you out as well.

Our strong preference is to land this as a good close to a 9 year chapter, while the relationship is still intact and everyone can feel good about how it ended.

Could we find time in the next week or two to meet and work out final terms? Once we're aligned, we'll put the agreement in writing and have it signed by both sides.

Thanks, Alex.
