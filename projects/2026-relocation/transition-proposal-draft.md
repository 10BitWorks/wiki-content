Hi Alex,

Thanks for taking the time to talk through our situation last week with John and Greg. While you continue the discussion with John, we wanted to follow up in writing with a documented statement of our intention not to renew, some context on where we stand, and a clear proposal for how we can meet each other's goals.

10BitWorks has kept going because of a community of members who donate time, skills, and money to the organization — operating without a single paid staff member. Yet, the organization maintaining that community has survived for over 15 years — even after the pandemic recession took the member count to new lows.

The Lachapelle warehouse has been that community's home for the better part of a decade, and we cannot overstate our appreciation for your stewardship and understanding. Increasingly, though, that same community has been telling us — through exits, declined memberships, and direct feedback — that the current space no longer works for them.

We — Connor, Greg, Eric, Ken, and Hunter — are Board members, but wear many other hats in the organization — and since our term started, have dedicated it especially toward capturing and addressing the common reasons members become ex-members. Wherever our volunteer force has the power to do something about it, we have. This was highly successful in stemming some of the bleeding: we're heading into summer with 20 more paid memberships than we started with, and have nearly recovered our size from right before the pandemic (when Ken became the 100th member). 

While that would have gotten us new spending power and exciting possibilities back in 2017, when we moved in at $0.38/SF — today, even with increased member dues, it means we now have just enough revenue to remain solvent as your 1-year sponsorship expires and we proceed with the lease rate of $1.00/SF. We thank you for your sponsorship - that temporary agreement likely made the difference for the continued existence of 10BitWorks in 2026, and our appreciation can't be overstated.

The Texas summer is arriving, though, along with the next inevitable wave of dropouts as our warehouse hits temperatures higher than the outdoors itself. While we're optimistically expecting better retention this time around, the cleanup, organization, beautification, support, and safety improvements can only get us so far. The best way for our nonprofit to sustain 300% higher rent is to achieve 300% more committed members, but the growth rate required defies our history in this space. Assuming it's eventually possible, we simply must continue to address the big reasons members leave... but so long as we remain in this industrial warehouse, the remaining reasons are not feasibly within our control.

The heat through most of the year, the inability to run classes at scale, and the lack of separation between dusty shop work and sensitive equipment are actively costing us members. The quality of the building's power infrastructure leaves several of our machines unreliable, and several that we can't use at all. The metal-shed construction drastically constrains our ability to make the aesthetic improvements needed to be on par with the more comfortable environment other makerspaces provide, or to rectify many of the security issues we've been bit with. We continue to battle flooding, pests, brownouts, and expensive water leaks, directing our volunteers' time and energy away from the things that get us new members. In full transparency, the organization has looked into relocating 10BitWorks on some occasions in years past. But with this combination of factors today, it's no longer a matter of convenience for us — it's what we need to do to stay viable. 

Thus, you can probably understand our decision. Whether we remain solvent until May 2027 or not, we won't be renewing our lease.


Having officially notified you of our intent not to renew, we also have a proposal for you, that would guarantee that 10BitWorks lives to see its 17th birthday.

Here's what we'd like to propose:

1.  Mutual termination of the lease effective July 31, 2026. That extends past our new space's readiness date and gives you two additional months of runway to line up a replacement tenant — addressing the timing concern you raised.
2.  Our improvements stay with the space. Over the years we've added substantial 110/220 electrical infrastructure and outlet capacity throughout our footprint — the kind of build-out any future maker, fabrication, or light-industrial tenant will value, and that's expensive to add from scratch.
3.  Broom-clean delivery with all rent current through July 31.
4.  Active tenant referrals from our network in the San Antonio maker, small-fabrication, and creative communities. We've surfaced qualified tenants for similar spaces before and are ready to put real effort into it here.

We also want to be straightforward with you: last year we made a mistake in committing to a two year lease rather than a single year, and in not negotiating a clear Early Termination clause like we had with our previous lease. We sincerely regret the position that puts you in. 

By shopping outside of the city center, though, we've found some locations that meet our needs, give us more room in our budget, and have agreed to our use case. We're excited about the potential here, and by negotiating an early termination with us, you would really help us out! While we don't have the funds for a termination fee beyond the two months additional rent, we want to continue to increase the value of your building and do whatever we can to help you out as well.

Our strong preference is to land this as a good close to a 9 year chapter, while the relationship is still intact and everyone can feel good about how it ended.

Could we find time in the next week or two to meet and work out final terms? Once we're aligned, we'll put the agreement in writing and have it signed by both sides.

Thanks, Alex.
