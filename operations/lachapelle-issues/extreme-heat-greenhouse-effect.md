Grievance: Extreme Heat, Operational Failure, and Seasonal Financial Unsustainability

Status: ACTIVE / RECURRING | Severity: Critical (Financial Survival & Operational Risk)

Description of the Environmental Failure

The facility at 130 W Lachapelle is a non-climate-controlled warehouse that lacks industrial-grade insulation and functional ventilation. During the summer months in San Antonio, the building shell acts as a "thermal trap," causing interior temperatures to consistently exceed outdoor levels. This creates a physically dangerous environment that has become the primary driver for membership attrition and the eventual relocation of the markerspace.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. The "Blisteringly Hot" Environment

Board members and general members have documented that the workshop becomes essentially unusable for nearly 33% of the year.

Transcript Account: [April 19 Meeting Transcript] emphasize that the warehouse becomes "blisteringly hot," and that members often choose to work anywhere else because the space is "hotter in here than outside."

Field Recording Verification: Members noted during the April 19 walkthrough: "It's just hotter in here than outside... that is one of our major dips in membership, is when it starts to get to a point where like it's blistering hot."

Physical Observation: During the walkthrough, members noted that even when the building's central HVAC (which is on the neighbor's side) is running, the makerspace receives zero benefit since it does not reach the 10BitWorks side.

2. Strategic Impact on 501(c)(3) Mission

The extreme heat prevents 10BitWorks from fulfilling its legal and tax-exempt mission as an educational institution.

Mission Stoppage: Classes cannot be safely hosted in the workshop during summer months due to the risk of heat-stroke for instructors and students.

Strategic Note: "The educational mission of this space is not being fulfilled in the current space right now... it's a club right now... we can't meet our legal obligation as a 501c3 [due to the environment]." [April 19 Transcript]

Revenue Impact: The makerspace experiences a "mass membership drive failure” every summer. Potential members who tour the facility in June, July, or August tend to decline joining once they experience the interior climate.

Financial Consequences

Membership Growth & Churn: Since the current Board took office in early 2025, membership has grown from a stagnant base in the high 60s (approx. 69 members) to a current peak of 89 members as of April 2026—a 29% increase in one year.

The Growth Deficit: While this growth is significant, it is insufficient for long-term survival at 130 W Lachapelle.

Strategic Quote: "The best way for our nonprofit to sustain 300% higher rent is to achieve 300% more committed members, but the growth rate required defies our history in this space." [Transition Proposal]

Sustainability Risk: While the current revenue from 89 members covers the $5,000 rent, any significant membership decline—a historical certainty starting in May—would result in an immediate financial deficit.

The "Hole in the Bucket": J. Devilbiss noted that trying to grow numbers in the current environment is futile: "Filling a bucket with water is futile if there's a big hole in the bottom... it will be much more difficult to increase our membership count if we don't relocate."

Quote: "We're about to hit those months where people drop... we'll start having a deficit as of this month and then it's literally just a matter of time before we're negative." [April 19 Transcript]

Lease Leverage & Actionable Breach

This environmental failure is the core justification for our "early termination" proposal.

Suitability for Purpose: Under Texas commercial law, a building must be suitable for the intended use of the Tenant. A warehouse shell that exceeds 100°F internally is not suitable for a public educational makerspace.

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks is prepared to argue that the Landlord’s failure to provide a building with basic industrial ventilation or insulation has rendered the premises "industrially uninhabitable," thereby breaching the "Quiet Enjoyment" of the space.

Strategic Demand: We are requesting a mutual termination of the lease by July 31, 2026, specifically to avoid the peak heat period that would otherwise force the organization into insolvency.
