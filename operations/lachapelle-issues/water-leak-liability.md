Grievance: $1,265.44 Water Leak Liability (Neighbor Fault)

Status: UNRESOLVED | Severity: High (Financial Breach)

Description

On September 10, 2025, 10BitWorks was forced to pay a massive water bill representing an approximately 80,000-gallon month. The leak was confirmed to be caused by a running toilet in an adjacent unit controlled by the Landlord and was actively mitigated by 10BitWorks members when property management failed to act.

Evidence & Verification

### The Mitigation (August 2025)

Direct Action: Connor Doherty and Hunter Wright proactively shut off the water to the entire facility (the whole side of the block) overnight to stop the massive waste event.

The Culprit: Connor and Hunter identified the source as a running toilet in a unit used for storage by the neighboring Tire company.

### Landlord Involvement

Delayed Response: The Landlord only got involved several days later by sending a plumber.

Secondary Failure: This plumber found and fixed a broken sump pump behind the cheerleader space where waste-water was flooding the ground—a separate maintenance failure under the Landlord's control.

### Financial Records

Cost Breakdown: August 2025 Water Leak - Sheet1.csv confirms the primary bill was $1,265.44 (paid Sept 10, 2025) for a total waste event valued at over $2,339.22.

SAWS Adjustment: SAWS provided a credit of $1,169.61 after the makerspace successfully proved the leak (at the neighbor's unit) had been fixed.

Alex's Direct Loss: The Landlord reportedly paid $850 for his own plumber's repair costs, proving he acknowledged the systemic failure on his side of the property.

Link: Eric Spehr confirmed the net cost to the space was $724.17 after internal reconciliations.

Landlord Neglect

Despite Connor and Hunter performing the initial mitigation to save the Landlord thousands in additional city fines and usage fees, Alex Bahrami refused to compensate the makerspace for the out-of-pocket hit. This incident serves as empirical proof that the Landlord is incapable of managing shared utilities fairly.

Actionable Claim

10BitWorks could seek a rent credit of $1,265.44 to recover the out-of-pocket loss caused by the Landlord's failure to maintain a secure and functional water system for his neighbors.
