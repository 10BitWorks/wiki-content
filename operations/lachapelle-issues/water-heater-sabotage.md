Issue: Obstructed Water Heater — Physical Prevention of Maintenance

Status: UNRESOLVED | Severity: High (Asset Damage & Loss of Essential Service)

Description

10BitWorks has been without functional hot water for several years. While Section 14 of the lease assigns system maintenance to the Tenant, the Landlord’s property management team (Ryan) has physically obstructed the makerspace's ability to maintain, repair, or replace the water heater.

Through the installation of new infrastructure for adjacent units, the Landlord created a physical barrier that prevents access to the utility unit, thereby "preventing performance" of the Tenant's maintenance obligations.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. Physical Obstruction during 2025 Renovation

During the plumbing additions for the new Glass Cutting unit in 2025, the Landlord’s crew routed a rigid PVC wastewater pipe directly across the exterior opening of the dedicated shed that houses 10BitWorks' water heater.

Walkthrough Verification (April 19, 2026): During the Field Recording walkthrough, board members visually confirmed the blockage: "There's our water heater that we can't get to because they put a pipe in front of it... [they] routed it right across the shed door, physically obstructing it."

Project Abandonment: As noted by Connor Doherty, Ryan was sent to provide plumbing for the neighbor, but he abandoned the site after the pipe installation, leaving the unit inaccessible and the project incomplete.

2. Resulting Systemic Damage

The prolonged lack of hot water has caused secondary damage to the building's plumbing infrastructure:

Backflow & Pressure Issues: Because the hot water lines have been empty for years, they have become a source of chronic backflow issues.

Bathroom Failure: Members report that whenever both valves are opened at a sink, the lack of pressure in the hot line causes waste-water leaks and persistent "smelly" floors in the toilet room.

Strategic Impact & Building Unsuitability

This issue serves as a critical counter-argument to any claim that 10BitWorks has failed its maintenance duties.

Legal Position: A Landlord cannot hold a Tenant liable for the failure of a system that the Landlord has made physically inaccessible through their own building modifications.

Demand for Remedy: 10BitWorks is documenting this as a primary example of how the Landlord’s "active interference" makes continued occupancy of the premises untenable.
