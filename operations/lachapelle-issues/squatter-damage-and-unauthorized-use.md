Grievance: Unauthorized Access, Squatter Activity, and Systemic Equipment Sabotage

Status: DORMANT | Severity: High (Asset Liability & Safety Risk)

Description of the Grievance

For nearly nine years, 10BitWorks has struggled with a chronic lack of building security caused directly by the Landlord's mismanagement of facility access. On multiple occasions, unauthorized individuals, "squatters," and un-vetted contractors have accessed the warehouse shell without notifying the Tenant. This has resulted in the repeated sabotage of industrial equipment, the theft of high-value assets, and the creation of a high-liability environment for markerspace members.

Detailed Evidence & Chronology

1. The 2018 Sabotage Wave (June 2018)

The Board documented a period of intense equipment damage specifically linked to individuals the Landlord allowed to "camp" or store materials in the common areas.

Verification: Terrence reported on June 14, 2018: "Unauthorized users have cut the power cords on the SawStop and left the sander arms loose... they have no respect for the tools or the safety protocols."

Unauthorized "Power Tapping": During this same period, "squatters" were documented using 10BitWorks' industrial power lines to run high-draw glass blowing kilns. These operations were conducted without firewalls, insurance, or the knowledge of the makerspace Board, creating a catastrophic fire risk.

2. The "Gaping Hole" and Camera Deployment (June 2018)

The Landlord’s failure to secure the building forced the Tenant to spend its own funds on a secondary security layer.

Discovery: Terrence documented a literal "gaping hole" in the building shell that remained unpatched for weeks.

Tenant Action: "All cameras are up, gaping hole, workspaces, and doors are covered. Just need a firewall hole for home viewing." [Terrence, June 29, 2018, Slack Archive]

3. The 400 lb Steel Hopper Theft (March 2026)

As recently as last month, the facility's lack of perimeter security resulted in the bold theft of a massive industrial component.

The Incident: A 400 lb steel hopper was stationed in front of the facility and stolen in broad daylight. This was along with a package for the neighbor, which had only been delivered 45 minutes earlier.

Verification: Board discussion on March 2, 2026: "Did that big metal thing out front get taken? ...missing 45 mins later... license JP or JF, rest is disfigured."

Security Failure: Despite 10BitWorks maintaining its own cameras, which caught the thieves, being a thin warehouse on Lachapelle makes our facility a target for industrial thieves.

4. Property Manager Intrusion (June 13, 2025)

The Landlord's crew has demonstrated a recurring lack of professional boundaries, sometimes being mistaken for actual burglars.

Verification: Connor Doherty reported: "They actually started cutting into the exterior side wall already since they couldn't get in... I got scared it was a break-in."

Consequences & Safety Risks

Asset Damage: 10BitWorks has incurred over $2,000 in tool repair costs (specifically SawStop cartridges and power line replacements) due to unauthorized handling.

Liability Risk: The Landlord allowing the former glassblowing neighbors to operate several large kilns on the 10BitWorks power grid created a safety and power management nightmare for the Board.

Lease Leverage & Actionable Breach

While Section 32 (No Security Service) of the lease attempts to waive Landlord liability for third-party theft, it does not cover the Landlord's Active Negligence in granting building access to un-vetted squatters and unauthorized users.
