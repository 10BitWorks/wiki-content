Issue: Unauthorized "Triple Daisy-Chain" Sewage Overload and System Interference

Status: ACTIVE | Severity: Critical (Biohazard & City Code Violation)

Description of the System Failure

The wastewater infrastructure at 130 W Lachapelle has been rendered hazardous through a series of unauthorized modifications sanctioned by the Landlord. While Section 14 of the lease assigns internal plumbing maintenance to the Tenant, the Landlord (via property manager Ryan) actively interfered with the system by "daisy-chaining" waste from multiple unrelated building units into 10BitWorks' internal sump pump.

This decision forces 10BitWorks to pay for the electrical and mechanical processing of other tenants' raw sewage, creating an illegal and non-compliant system overload.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. The Physical Configuration (Triple Daisy-Chain)

During the April 19 walkthrough, members documented the specific path of wastewater:

The Chain: Waste from the newest tenant (Glass Cutting / Window Installation) is pumped into 10BitWorks' internal sump pump. This combined load is then pumped into a third unit (the former Cheerleader space), where a final pump attempts to move the mass to the street-level sewer.

Walkthrough Quote: "When you say a sump pump is it a grinder pump that moves all the waste uphill... It's all wastewater. Right. Shit water that's going to the main sewer line... it's literally a bucket out in the open." [Field Recording]

Technical Observation: The system relies on "macerating" or grinding pumps that must move waste "uphill" through three separate stages. By forcing neighbor waste into our pump, the Landlord has compromised the mechanical integrity of our internal plumbing responsibility.

2. Documented Failures & "Sewage Scandals"

### The system's chronic over-capacity has led to several failures

The Block Scandal: Configuration failures have led to multiple "sewage overflow scandals" where waste reached the street level, drawing negative attention from the surrounding neighborhood.

Drain Backups: Waste-water frequently backs up through the floor drains on the patio.

Direct Quote: "The sewer when it was overflowing would come up and be really stinky through the one little drain..." [Field Recording]

The Freeze Failure (Jan 2026): During the winter freeze, the exposed "daisy-chain" lines behind the building (Landlord-installed) froze solid, preventing any use of the facility’s bathrooms.

Landlord Action & Liability

Property management (Ryan) was explicitly notified that this configuration was unsuitable.

Verification: As Connor Doherty noted, Ryan claimed he was sent to "give them a bathroom and separate plumbing," but instead of installing a dedicated line to the street, he simply "daisy-chained it to ours."

Active Interference: This is not a "maintenance" failure by the Tenant, but an "active intervention" by the Landlord that creates a biohazard risk.

Strategic Impact & Actionable Breach

This configuration is a direct violation of International Plumbing Code (IPC) and San Antonio city ordinances regarding the isolation of commercial sewer lines.

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks is prepared to file a formal complaint with the City of San Antonio Development Services (Plumbing Inspection). This would likely result in a "Red Tag" order for the entire building.

Settlement Offer: We will withhold this report to the city in exchange for a mutual, no-fee termination of our lease by July 31, 2026.
