Grievance: WarHouse Gym Conflict, Harassment, and Landlord Favoritism

Status: ACTIVE | Severity: Low (Operational & Membership Attrition)

Description of the Grievance

10BitWorks has endured a pattern of harassment, intimidation, and illegal property encroachment by the neighboring tenant, WarHouse Gym. This conflict has been significantly exacerbated by the Landlord's (Alex Bahrami) refusal to intervene or enforce commercial boundaries, presumably due to the gym’s status as a "high-dollar" tenant.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. The "Bouncer-Style" Intimidation Incident

Makerspace members have been directly confronted and harassed by gym staff regarding the use of shared parking facilities.

Transcript Account: Connor Doherty documented a specific encounter: "The wife of the owner... came over with like a big bouncer guy and was like, 'Hey, you know... we noticed that you were here overnight and it's making our members uncomfortable, you can't park here.'"

False Claims: During this intimidation attempt, the gym owners falsely claimed they had "worked with the town to expand this parking lot." In reality, the town was upset that they had appropriated the public land, and had given no such permission, let alone collaboration.

2. Illegal Encroachment into Public Greenway

The gym aggressively expanded its footprint by illegally fencing out past its property lines and into the town's public greenway and right-of-way.

Impact on the City: The gym’s unauthorized fencing blocked a planned trail connection intended to link to the city's greenway system.

City Retaliation: Eventually, the town forced the gym to "retreat with their tail between their legs." City construction crews arrived, took down the illegal fencing, and put up cones to mark the actual property boundary.

Persistence: Despite the city's intervention, a partial fence remains that continues to block the end of the public trail.

3. "Sweetheart" Treatment and Landlord Favoritism

The Board has identified a clear pattern of Alex Bahrami favoring the gym at the expense of all other tenants on the block.

Direct Quote: "The gym is Alex's sweetheart because they pay more than all of us. Whenever something happens on the block that the gym doesn't like, they come out pretty rudely and loudly... he treats them as his darling tenant." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Parking Inequity: On Saturdays, the block becomes "chaos" as gym members overload the entire parking lot. The Landlord has failed to enforce any parking allocation, allowing the gym to push 10BitWorks members out of spaces they pay for as part of their triple-net lease.

Consequences & Damages

Membership Attrition: Potential members have cited the "hostile environment" and the "heavy handed" approach of the gym neighbors as a primary reason for not joining 10BitWorks.

Safety Concerns: The presence of aggressive staff and bouncer-style intimidation has made evening and weekend visits uncomfortable for many makerspace members, particularly women. [Meeting Transcript]

Strategic Impact & Actionable Breach

The Landlord’s refusal to manage his "darling tenant" constitutes a material breach of the Tenant's right to "Quiet and Peaceful Enjoyment" of the premises.

Legal Argument: A Landlord is required to ensure that the actions of one tenant do not interfere with the legitimate operations of another. By granting the gym de facto control over the parking lot and ignoring their harassment of our members, Alex Bahrami has breached this duty.

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks can use this documented pattern of harassment as further justification for an immediate, mutual lease termination. We can argue that the building environment has become hostile to our educational mission.
