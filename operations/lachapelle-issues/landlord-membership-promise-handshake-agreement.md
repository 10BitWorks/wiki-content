Grievance: Broken $1,000/Month Membership Promise and Resulting Manual Rent Deductions

Status: ACTIVE / ONGOING | Severity: High (Critical Financial Sustainability)

Description of the Strategic Arrangement

During the 2025 lease negotiations, 10BitWorks was forced to accept a significant rent increase to $1.00 per square foot for a reduced footprint of 5,000 sq ft. To make this high rate financially viable for the non-profit, the Landlord (Alex Bahrami) verbally entered into a "handshake agreement." Alex proposed to "subscribe" as a corporate member for $1,000 per month, which was intended to act as an under-the-table rent discount or offset.

However, the Landlord failed to fulfill the recurring nature of this promise, creating a systemic financial instability that forced the 10BitWorks Board into proactive defensive measures.

Detailed Evidence & Chronology

1. The Failure of the "Automatic" Subscription

Records and board testimony confirm that the Landlord never established the promised recurring membership payment.

The Single Payment: Alex Bahrami made a one-time donation of $1,000 on August 19, 2025. [Finance Records / Board Discussion]

The "Chase" Period (Aug - Sept 2025): For the subsequent months, board members were forced to repeatedly "chase" the Landlord for individual donations.

Transcript Quote: "Alex said that he would subscribe as a member for a thousand dollars a month... it turns out that he just gave a one time donation and we would have to go after him every month for that donation." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Growth Context: Despite this financial struggle, the Board’s focus on membership retention and cleanup resulted in a growth from high 60s (approx. 69 members) in early 2025 to a current peak of 89 members—a 29% increase under the current term.

2. The Treasurer's Recourse (October 2025 - Present)

In October 2025, Treasurer Eric Spehr made a unilateral decision to formalize the discount since the Landlord was failing to provide the funds voluntarily.

Direct Quote: "Eric got tired of that and just started deducting a thousand dollars from what we pay them and we haven't heard anything." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Financial Verification: The Transaction Export shows the transition.

September 2025 Rent: Approximately $4,045.14.

October 2025 Rent: Approximately $3,049.82, reflecting the initiation of the manual $1,000 deduction.

Landlord Neglect & Lack of Oversight

The Landlord’s team has failed to notice or flag these recurring $1,000 deductions for over six months.

Strategic Observation: "It is very likely that if we kept doing that they wouldn't notice. I don't know if it's his assistants or whatever that actually interact with our payments directly… there's a lot of fudging that goes into the numbers." [Field Recording]

Strategic Risk & Lease Leverage

This arrangement poses both a risk and an opportunity in our current move strategy:

The Contractual Vulnerability: Because the $1,000 discount was a "handshake agreement" and is not codified in the written lease, the Landlord could technically claim a rent deficiency of $7,000+ if negotiations turn hostile.

The "Handshake" Precedent: We could use the Landlord's history of "handshake" modifications (including the utility sharing agreement) to argue that the verbal discount is a binding modification of the payment terms.

Mission Criticality: Without this $1,000/month credit, 10BitWorks cannot remain solvent during the seasonal membership dip caused by the warehouse heat. The Board can argue that the $1.00/sq ft rate was only ever viable under the condition of the Landlord's sponsorship.
