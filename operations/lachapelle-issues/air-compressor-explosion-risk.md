Issue: End-of-Life Industrial Infrastructure — Air Compressor Liability

Status: OBSELETE | Severity: High (Safety/Explosion Hazard)

Description of the Risk

The building’s legacy air compressor—a critical piece of industrial infrastructure provided with the space—is in a state of terminal failure. Internal corrosion from years of pre-existing moisture accumulation has reached a point where the unit is rusting from the inside out, making it a liability for any tenant.

Evidence & Verification

### Safety Report (June 11, 2024)

Link: Garrett Bagwell reported on June 11, 2024: "The large air compressor was never drained... it is rusted on the inside, posing a catastrophic explosion risk to anyone in the vicinity."

Operational Stoppage: Because the compressor is an industrial pressure vessel, the presence of internal corrosion makes it a "ticking time bomb." The Board has had to restrict usage of the unit, hindering workshop operations.

Legacy Defect (April 19, 2026): During the walk-around, members noted that the compressor circuit continues to fail. The unit’s degraded state predates the current board and represents a significant capital replacement cost that the makerspace cannot justify under the current rent rate.

Field Recording Quote: "Just hoping that the circuit panel is nicely labeled... shouldn't be too difficult to find the air compressor circuit." (Austen Hoogen regarding recurring power failures to the unit).

Consequences

Life-Safety Risk: A pressurized air tank that is compromised by rust can fail explosively, sending shrapnel through the warehouse.

Tool Downtime: The lack of reliable air pressure has rendered many of the makerspace’s pneumatic tools unusable.

In early 2026, Hunter Wright donated a new air compressor surpassing the size of all previous machines, which has become the new default.
