Grievance: 9-Year Failure to Provide Separate Utility Metering (Systemic Management Neglect)

Status: UNRESOLVED | Severity: High (Administrative, Financial & Operational Burden)

Description of the Grievance

Since the makerspace first occupied 130 W Lachapelle in July 2017, a core condition of the tenancy was the installation of independent utility meters (electric and water) for each unit. For nearly nine years, the Landlord (Alex Bahrami) and his property management teams (Nick, Ryan) have repeatedly promised to install these meters.

As of April 2026, these promises remain unfulfilled. This has forced 10BitWorks into the involuntary and unpaid role of "Block Utility Manager," requiring the non-profit to subsidize the Landlord’s other tenants and manually resolve complex billing disputes.

Detailed Chronology of Broken Promises

1. Initial Move-In (July 2017)

The original Board signed the first lease based on the Landlord’s verbal assurance that separate meters would be installed "within months" to accommodate the planned subdivision of the warehouse.

2. Formal Board Inquiry (January 3, 2018)

Six months into the lease, 10BitWorks sent a formal notice to Alex Bahrami regarding the lack of progress:

Direct Quote: "When will the new meter be installed? Since the other tenants have moved in we have been paying all of the electrical cost, this is not fair... I will follow up with a phone call soon." [Email Archive]

3. The "Idiotic Agreement" (September 2018)

By late 2018, the lack of meters had forced a "handshake" power-sharing agreement that board members described as non-functional and biased.

Evidence: Terrence reported in Sept 2018 that the current arrangement was "extremely unfair and was only slightly in our favor due to Alex's idiotic power sharing agreement. If we didn't have that in place I think we would've lost money."

4. Property Management Assurance (October 2018)

Property manager Ryan specifically claimed that the 2018 renovation (wall installation) would include the meter separation.

Verification: Joe Rogers documented: "They are separating electrical meters for the building... replacing lights with LEDS." (The lights were replaced by 10BitWorks members in 2025, but the meters never were).

The Administrative Burden of "Fudged" Calculations

Because no professional metering exists, 10BitWorks is forced to use its own capital to monitor the Landlord's other tenants.

The Emporia System: 10BitWorks purchased and installed its own consumer-grade IoT sensors (Emporia) to attempt to track which circuits are being used by neighbors.

Manual Bookkeeping: Every month, the Board must perform complex math to subtract neighbor usage from the building's main bill. This process is described as a source of "constant anxiety and stress."

Field Recording Quote: "We've just been trusted to do all the math ourselves... so there's a lot of fudging that goes into the numbers of what we pay."

Direct Deposit Stress: For years, members had to "physically carry a direct deposit or cashier's check over to [Alex's] bank" just to ensure the complex bill-rent-discount math was accepted.

Strategic Impact & Actionable Breach

The lack of separate meters, in the case of the water bill, directly led to the $1,265.44 loss in September 2025. Had a separate meter been in place, the 80,000-gallon leak in the neighbor's unit would not have been billed to the makerspace.

Strategic Note: 10BitWorks can now demand that the Landlord fulfill the 9-year-old promise to install separate meters within 30 days or provide a permanent, written monthly credit of $250 to compensate for the administrative labor and financial risk incurred by the Tenant.

Actionable Claim: The Landlord's failure to provide standard utility separation constitutes a breach of the "Premises" suitability and has caused significant financial harm to the Tenant.
