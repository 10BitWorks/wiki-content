Grievance: Systemic Dumpster Mismanagement and Neighboring "Theft of Service"

Status: RESOLVED WITH WORKAROUND | Severity: Medium (Financial & Administrative Liability)

Description of the Grievance

For over seven years, 10BitWorks has been subjected to a recurring "theft of service" regarding its private waste disposal. Neighboring tenants—specifically WarHouse Gym and the former "Cheerleader" movers—have consistently used the makerspace’s private dumpster to dispose of their own industrial, commercial, and personal waste.

The Landlord (Alex Bahrami) has failed to provide a secure disposal area or enforce waste agreements among his tenants, forcing 10BitWorks to subsidize the waste disposal costs of the entire commercial block.

Detailed Evidence & Chronology

1. Historical Precedent (August 2019)

The issue of unauthorized dumpster usage was first formally documented by the Board in the summer of 2019.

Verification: Donsmeller reported in August 2019: "Any news on the dumpster? ...movers may have some stuff in storage there that is infested. I can send a message to Alex."

Context: At the time, the neighboring moving company was using 10BitWorks' bin to dispose of infested materials, potentially introducing pests (rats/roaches) into the makerspace's primary disposal point.

2. Persistent Usage Theft (2024 - 2026)

In the current lease term, the Neighboring Gym has become the primary source of unauthorized usage.

Transcript Quote: "Neighbors frequently fill 10BitWorks' dumpster with their own industrial waste, leading to overflows and additional costs. This has been reported since August 2019 with no resolution from the Landlord." [Meeting Summary]

Direct Observation: 10BitWorks members have documented gym employees walking across the shared parking lot to deposit large volumes of trash into our bin, effectively bypassing their own service requirements (or lack thereof).

Impact: This usage causes the dumpster to reach capacity days before its scheduled pickup, leading to unsightly overflows at the entrance of the facility and attracting local wildlife (possums/rats).

Financial Impact on the Non-Profit

Overage Fees: 10BitWorks has incurred multiple "overage" or "extra pickup" fees from its waste provider because the bin is consistently filled by third parties.

Administrative Labor: The Board has had to spend dozens of hours over seven years attempting to coordinate "lock" systems or confront neighbors, a task that should be managed by the property owner.

Landlord Neglect

Alex Bahrami has been notified of this "theft of service" multiple times.

Impact

In 2024, Juan implemented a lock on the dumpster. While this has not been consistently installed in 2026, there have been no new instances of outsiders using our dumpster.
