Grievance: Exterior Wall Integrity ("Swiss Cheese" Siding) and Resulting Infestations

Status: UNRESOLVED | Severity: High (Structural, Environmental & Health)

Description of the Grievance

The building shell at 130 W Lachapelle has suffered from a decade of deferred maintenance, resulting in exterior walls that are structurally unsound and physically compromised. Members frequently refer to the siding as "Swiss cheese" due to the numerous holes and gaps that allow for the free exchange of air, moisture, and pests.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. Primary Documentation of Defects (Feb 2025)

The structural failure of the building shell was formally identified as a primary grievance in February 2025.

Verification: J. Devilbiss reported that "the interested potential tenant probably hasn't realized all the maintenance problems... exterior metal flashing breaking loose."

Transcript Evidence: During the board meeting on April 19, 2026, members emphasized that these gaps make it impossible to climate control the space.

Landlord Damage: During the 2025 renovation, the Landlord's crew caused fresh damage to the siding while attempting to force entry without keys. [Connor Doherty, #general, June 13, 2025]

2. Chronic Pest Infestations (Rats and Possums)

The structural gaps have directly led to hazardous pest activity in the makerspace’s main administrative areas.

Verification: J. Devilbiss confirmed in Feb 2025: "Rats get into the office walls... A possum slept over the ceiling of the upper office space."

Direct Link: This infestation is a direct result of the Landlord's failure to provide a weather-tight and secure building shell as required by industrial code.

Consequences & Safety Risks

Health Hazard: The presence of nesting wildlife in the office ceilings creates an unsanitary environment for staff and board members.

Environment Failure: The lack of a proper seal renders the workshop "hotter inside than outside" during San Antonio summers.

Lease Leverage & Actionable Breach

The maintenance of the building shell is one of the few items the Landlord cannot delegate under the current agreement.

Section 13 (Landlord's Repairs): Mandates Landlord responsibility for the "Exterior Walls."

Actionable Claim: The Landlord is in clear breach of Section 13. We are prepared to argue that the building is "industrially uninhabitable" due to the structural porousness of the siding.
