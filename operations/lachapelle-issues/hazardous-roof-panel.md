Grievance: Hazardous and Unsecured Roof Panel (Life-Safety Hazard)

Status: UNRESOLVED | Severity: Critical (Life-Safety)

Description of Hazard

The industrial facility at 130 W Lachapelle utilizes a "double roof" architecture, consisting of a secondary corrugated metal skin installed over the building's original roof. A massive metal panel on the upper section of this secondary roof is currently unsecured and at least 70% detached from its structural anchors. During San Antonio's frequent high-wind events and thunderstorms, this panel acts as a sail, catching the wind before slamming down with extreme force onto the building's internal ceiling structure.

Evidence & Direct Observations

1. Physical Walkthrough (April 19, 2026)

During a board-led inspection of the facility perimeter and interior, the following was documented:

Acoustic Evidence: The panel is picked up by strong winds from one direction, then is slammed back down from wind in the other direction, producing a distinct and alarming crashing sound that resonates throughout the workshop.

Visual Discrepancy: The damage is deceptive from the interior. As noted in the Field Recording: "You go in to the corner and you look up and you're like, well, I don't see the roof ripping off, but you go outside and you're like, the roof's ripping off."

Historical Failure Proof: Board members noted that a similar panel on the adjacent side of the building has already been "ripped off" by wind, establishing a clear precedent for the eventual failure of the remaining unsecured panel.

2. Board Meeting Testimony (April 19, 2026)

The Board of Directors has characterized this as a life-safety issue rather than a simple maintenance request:

Quote: "That giant metal panel on the roof that slams down during every storm... it is definitely the kind of thing that could kill someone outside if it does [detach]." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Impact: The force of the panel slamming is described as a "rapid-fire" booming that can be felt in the floor of the workshop, indicating significant kinetic energy that the building's internal ceiling was not designed to absorb.

Documented Neglect & Broken Promises

This structural failure has been a known issue for years and has been reported to the Landlord’s property management team (specifically Ryan) on multiple occasions.

The "Maintenance" Façade: Property management has previously claimed that crews were sent to "look at the roof." However, 10BitWorks members have observed no physical evidence of repair, and the panel continues to slam during every storm.

Communication Failure: Despite the potential for lethal injury should the panel detach or collapse the ceiling onto members below, the Landlord (Alex Bahrami) has failed to provide a timeline for a permanent fix or a professional engineering assessment.

Lease Leverage & Actionable Breach

This failure is a direct and unambiguous breach of the Landlord's primary responsibility under the current 2025 Lease agreement.

Section 13 (Landlord's Repairs): The lease states that "Landlord shall have no obligations under this Lease to repair or maintain any portion of the Premises... other than the Roof and Exterior Walls."

Strategic Positioning: Since the roof is one of the only two areas the Landlord is contractually obligated to maintain, his failure to secure this panel constitutes a material breach of the lease.

Demand for Remedy: 10BitWorks can issue a formal notice that this life-safety hazard must be permanently repaired by a certified roofing contractor within 30 days, as per the "Notice of Default" provisions in the lease.
