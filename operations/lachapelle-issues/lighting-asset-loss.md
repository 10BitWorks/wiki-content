Grievance: Loss of Lighting Assets in Subdivided Space

Status: ACTIVE | Severity: Medium (Property Loss, Electricity Waste)

Description

During the 2025 space reduction, 10BitWorks lost possession and control of nearly half of its massive array of high-quality LED lighting fixtures (20+ tubes). These fixtures were purchased and installed by 10BitWorks members to replace the building's original, failing fluorescent lighting.

Evidence & Verification

### The Installation Project (Dec 2024 - Jan 2025)

Primary Contributor: The replacement project was a weeks-long effort primarily led by member Jon (Jonathan) Harris, with significant physical assistance from Connor Doherty.

Detail: The project involved replacing 20+ fluorescent tubes throughout the workshop area. Jon and Connor used scaffolding to access the high industrial ceilings, as standard ladders were insufficient for the height.

Technical Work: Jon meticulously performed ballast bypass conversions on the original fixtures to allow for direct-wire T8 LED tube compatibility.

Completion Documentation: Connor Doherty reported on January 13, 2025: "The lights are replaced and the shelves are moved back."

Member Impact: Member Carmela Muguerza noted the improvement on January 15, 2025: "Thanks Good lighting."

The Asset Trap (2025 Subdivision)

Following the footprint reduction in 2025, these specific fixtures—which represent hundreds of dollars in materials and dozens of hours of volunteer labor—were left in the space now occupied by the glass-cutting company.

Property Manager Failure: Property manager Ryan promised to return the 10BitWorks lights during the subdivision process, but he abandoned the site before doing so.

Blocked Recovery: Members cannot manually recover this lighting equipment because the space is now occupied by a new tenant.

Ongoing Utility Theft: Because the Landlord failed to isolate the electrical circuits, 10BitWorks is currently paying the electricity bill for some of the lights Jon and Connor installed, which are now lighting the area occupied by the neighbor.

Verification: "Whenever we turn on the lights here we're lighting up their space even when they're not here." [Field Recording, April 19, 2026]

Actionable Claim

10BitWorks is seeking the immediate return of the 20+ LED fixtures or a credit of $1,500 (reflecting material costs and the specialized labor involved in the scaffolding-based installation and ballast bypass) against the remaining rent.
