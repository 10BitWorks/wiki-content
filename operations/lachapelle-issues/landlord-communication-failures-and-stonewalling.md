Grievance: Systemic Communication Failures, Property Manager Neglect, and Landlord Stonewalling

Status: PERSISTENT / LONG-TERM | Severity: High (Operational Obstruction & Bad Faith)

Description of the Grievance

A documented, multi-year pattern of non-responsiveness and "stonewalling" from the Landlord (Alex Bahrami) and his property managers (Nick, Ryan) has forced 10BitWorks into extreme administrative measures to secure even basic facility management. This neglect has directly prevented necessary repairs, caused financial instability, and forced the non-profit Board to spend hundreds of hours on defensive documentation rather than its primary mission.

Detailed Evidence & Chronology

1. The "Certified Letter" Precedent (July 2018)

The Landlord’s pattern of "going dark" is not a new phenomenon; it has been a core feature of his management style since the first lease signing.

Verification: Ken Runner documented in July 2018: "Alex and Nick have both gone dark for 10 days now... I am going to have to send a certified letter to get a response regarding our lease status."

Context: This escalation was the only way the previous Board was able to get the Landlord to acknowledge the makerspace's existence or the terms of their agreement.

2. Disappearance of Property Management (2025 Renovation)

During the critical period of the building’s subdivision and 10BitWorks' footprint reduction, property manager Ryan was assigned to oversee infrastructure isolation.

Transcript Quote: "Ryan was sent here... to put up a wall and to give them a bathroom... and also to run new power so that they have separate power. Also to do new separate plumbing... something happened where Ryan just stopped coming back and then it was vacant for a while." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Resulting Harm: Because Ryan abandoned the site, the separate meters were never installed, the 10BitWorks lights were never returned, and the "Triple Daisy-Chain" plumbing was left in a hazardous, non-compliant state.

3. Board Frustration & Response Tactics (April 2026)

The current Board continues to face the same lack of professionalism. Members have had to resort to "bullshit" tactics just to get the Landlord to answer his phone.

Field Recording Quote: "He [Alex] went dark for his dark on the scroll... I said we've got a board meeting on such and such night... it was bullshit, but it was essentially trying to get him to answer." [John Chadwell]

Response Delay: Board members noted in the Meeting Transcript that even during high-stakes lease negotiations, the Landlord remains unreachable for weeks at a time, often responding only after multiple "nudges."

4. Chasing Monthly Obligations (Aug - Oct 2025)

The Landlord’s communication failure extends to his own financial commitments to the Tenant.

Verification: For three consecutive months, the Board had to manually contact Alex Bahrami via text and email to "chase down" the agreed-upon $1,000 monthly subscription donation.

The Recourse: This failure to maintain an automated system is what led to the manual rent deductions starting in October 2025.

Consequences & Strategic Impact

Deferred Maintenance: Critical issues like the hazardous roof panel and leaking sewer line remain unaddressed because there is no reliable reporting mechanism that results in action.

Involuntary "Manager" Role: The Landlord’s non-responsiveness has forced 10BitWorks into the role of "unpaid property manager" for the entire building, handling neighbor disputes and utility math that should be the Landlord’s job.

Operational Risk: The makerspace cannot sign a new lease at its target location because the Landlord refuses to provide a clear, written exit strategy or timeline.

Actionable Claim & Leverage

The Landlord's multi-year pattern of non-communication and "stonewalling" is a breach of the Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing present in all commercial contracts.

Strategic Demand: 10BitWorks could issue a final formal notice that all future communications must be acknowledged in writing within 48 hours.

Termination Trigger: We can argue that the Landlord’s persistent "disappearance" has made the building unmanageable and uninsurable, providing legal justification for an immediate, mutual lease termination by July 31, 2026.
