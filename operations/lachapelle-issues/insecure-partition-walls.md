Issue: Low-Effort Subdivision and Insecure Partitioning

Status: ACTIVE | Severity: High (Operational & Asset Loss)

Description of the Subdivision Failure

Since 2017, the Landlord (Alex Bahrami) has subdivided the warehouse at 130 W Lachapelle into increasingly smaller units to maximize rental income. These subdivisions have been executed with disregard for existing infrastructure or Tenant operations, forcing 10BitWorks into multiple rounds of uncompensated capital expenditures and permanent security compromises.

1. The 2018 Cheerleader Partition (North Side)

A wall was installed around 2018 to separate 10BitWorks from the space occupied by the "Cheerleader school."

Second-floor Interference: The partition was positioned such that it completely cut off 10BitWorks from the building's original central staircase.

Forced Build-out: To maintain access to our upstairs office and flex rooms, 10BitWorks was forced to build a new, adjacent metal staircase at its own expense. The Landlord provided no credit or assistance for this mandatory structural addition.

HVAC Control Loss: This wall also cut 10BitWorks off from the building's central HVAC controls, which remained on the Cheerleader side. We continued to pay the electricity bill for a system we could no longer control.

Electrical Loss: This subdivision cut 10BitWorks off from its main power panel and the big exhaust fans.

2. The 2025 Glass Cutting Side Wall (South Side)

Following a 2025 footprint reduction, a new wall was installed on the end of the building near the patio.

Asset Loss: High-quality LED lighting fixtures (20+ tubes) installed by Jon Harris and Connor Doherty are now trapped in the neighbor's space.

Direct Quote: "10BitWorks cannot manually recover their lighting equipment since the space is now occupied... property manager Ryan said he would take them down but just stopped coming back." [April 19 Meeting Transcript]

Security Failure: The wall was "too short" to meet the roof line, creating a gap large enough for unauthorized entry between the units.

3. Patio and Sliding Door Design Flaws

The subdivision process effectively abandoned the security of the shared patio area.

Structural Gap: As J. Devilbiss confirmed, a significant gap exists below the patio sliding door, which cannot be secured against pests or unauthorized entry.

Strategic Impact & Building Unsuitability

The Landlord’s haphazard subdivision has rendered the premises "unfit for the purpose of a secure workshop."

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks could seek a reimbursement of $5,000+ for the cost of the forced staircase build-out and the loss of the lighting assets as part of a final settlement.

Strategic Position: We suggest to Alex that the North side wall be removed upon our departure to attract a single, higher-paying tenant who can use the combined 10,000+ sq ft footprint coherently. This serves as a "goodwill" negotiation point for our early exit.
