Grievance: Systemic Electrical Failures, Grid Instability, and Unauthorized Power Tapping

Status: ONGOING | Severity: Critical

Description of the Electrical Compromise

The electrical infrastructure at 130 W Lachapelle is fundamentally unsafe, improperly balanced for industrial operations, and has been subject to unauthorized "tapping" by the Landlord to subsidize other tenants. These issues have resulted in recurring operational stoppages, equipment damage, and the involuntary subsidization of neighboring businesses.

Detailed Evidence & Verification

1. Chronic Grid Instability ("The Kiln Freak Out")

The makerspace experiences total grid instability whenever high-draw industrial equipment (specifically ceramics kilns) is in operation.

Verification: Connor Doherty reported on March 25, 2026: "When the Kilns are on at night and start doing their rapid on-off thing, our whole power grid freaks out... lights flicker and even the UPS is activated."

Operational Risk: This instability has caused sensitive electronic equipment (computers, laser cutters, 3D printers) to crash or suffer damage. The constant flickering of lights is described as "unsettling" during late-night workshop sessions.

Faulty Outlets: Members have documented "dead sockets" that remain non-functional despite repeated GFI resets, indicating underlying structural wiring failures. [Greg Thibodeaux, #general, Feb 12, 2026]

2. Unauthorized 3-Phase Tapping and Capacity Theft

For nearly eight years, the Landlord has used 10BitWorks' electrical infrastructure to power unrelated building units.

Historical Discovery (June 2018): The Board documented that one entire leg of the facility's 3-phase industrial power was being "tapped" to provide electricity to the building's common areas and other tenant units.

Direct Impact: This unauthorized usage prevented 10BitWorks from installing new heavy machinery, as the Landlord's "theft of capacity" left the makerspace with insufficient amperage for its own documented needs.

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks could seek an audit of the building's electrical load to determine the exact value of the capacity "stolen" by the Landlord since 2018.

3. Lighting Mis-wiring and Utility Theft (2025 Subdivision)

Following the 2025 space reduction, the Landlord failed to properly isolate the electrical circuits for the new units.

Verification: Board members documented in April 2026 that "New lights were installed in the adjacent space but wired to 10BitWorks power, meaning the makerspace is effectively paying to light their neighbor's unit."

Direct Quote: "Whenever we turn on the lights here we're lighting up their space even when they're not here." [Field Recording, April 19, 2026]

Asset Loss: The Landlord further failed to return high-quality LED fixtures (20+ tubes) that Jon Harris and Connor Doherty installed, leaving them wired to the makerspace’s meter but physically located in the neighbor's occupied space.

4. HVAC Control Denial (Paid for, but Inaccessible)

10BitWorks remains electrically responsible for the building's central HVAC system, yet has been physically cut off from the controls.

The Conflict: The 2025 partition wall placed the thermostat and unit on the neighbor's side. However, the system is still wired to 10BitWorks' main panel.

Observation: Cold air is confirmed to blow from a vent in the upstairs bathroom when the neighbor uses the system, proving that 10BitWorks is paying to air-condition a space it can no longer access.

Strategic Impact & Actionable Breach

The Landlord is in clear violation of Section 13 (Landlord's Repairs) regarding the maintenance of the "Exterior Shell" and utility suitability.

Code Violation: The unauthorized tapping and mis-wiring of commercial units constitute a direct violation of the National Electrical Code (NEC) regarding individual tenant metering and isolation.

Actionable Claim: 10BitWorks could seek an immediate and permanent isolation of its electrical grid or a $5,000 credit to cover the estimated utility theft and capacity loss incurred over the life of the lease.

Strategic Offer: We propose that the Landlord instead allow for an early, mutual lease termination to avoid a formal building inspection by city electrical authorities.
